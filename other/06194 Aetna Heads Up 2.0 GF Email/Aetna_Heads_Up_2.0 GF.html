Be prepared for 2016 Renewal time

You should have recently received a letter with detailed information about how to enroll in a 2016 health plan. Make sure to read the letter so you know what you need to do to make sure you continue to have health coverage in 2016.

The Affordable Care Act (ACA) has been changing health insurance

ACA Plans now include preventive care and other essential health benefits. Plans prior to the start of the Affordable Care Act, also called grandfathered plans, don’t have many of the standard benefits of ACA plans.1

Your particular grandfathered health insurance plan will no longer be offered. Your current coverage will end on December 31, 2015 and you will need to select a new ACA health plan for 2016.

Get the most out of your health care coverage

Now more than ever, it’s important that you maintain the right health care coverage to avoid gaps in coverage – or any tax penalties. We’re here for you every step of the way:

• For over 160 years we’ve been committed to our members’ well-being.
• We want to help you identify the best coverage for you and your family’s needs.

Questions?

If you have any questions about your current plan, you can contact Member Services at the toll-free phone number on the back of your Member ID card.

  

2016 Renewal: Important dates to remember
 

November 1, 2015:

2016 Open Enrollment begins

This is the first day you can shop and enroll in a 2016 health insurance plan.

December 15, 2015:2

Last day to renew or choose a plan

Make sure coverage will start on January 1, 2016.

January 1, 2016:

First date 2016 health insurance coverage begins

This is the plan effective date.

January 31, 2016:

2016 Open Enrollment ends

This is the last day you can make changes or enroll in a new 2016 health insurance plan, without potential penalty for being uninsured.
